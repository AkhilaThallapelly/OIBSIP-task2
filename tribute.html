Swami Vivekananda

A Tribute to the Great Philosopher and Spiritual Leader

About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863 – 1902) was an Indian Hindu monk, a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He is best known for his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, where he began with the words "Sisters and brothers of America...," and his teachings of universal brotherhood.

Vivekananda played a major role in the revival of Hinduism in India, and his works inspired many to pursue spiritual enlightenment. His message of self-reliance, mental strength, and service to humanity continues to inspire people across the world.

His Teachings

Vivekananda emphasized the importance of education, spiritual development, and unity among people. He believed that real progress could only be achieved through self-discipline, determination, and an awareness of our divine nature. His teachings are still regarded as essential for spiritual development and are considered a guiding light for personal growth and social change.

Legacy

Swami Vivekananda passed away on July 4, 1902, but his legacy lives on. His thoughts and works are remembered for their emphasis on service, spiritual wisdom, and national pride. His contributions to modern thought and philosophy have left an indelible mark on society, both in India and globally.
